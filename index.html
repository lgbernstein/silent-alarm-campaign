Campaign Overview

Executive Summary

The CPAP/BiPAP market growth has stagnated due to low OSA awareness and diagnosis rates. Our strategy directly addresses this by targeting the most influential stakeholders - the spouses of potential OSA sufferers - through an empathetic, multi-channel campaign that reframes OSA as a serious but treatable condition.

Market Challenge

Despite 25 million Americans suffering from OSA, diagnosis rates remain below 20%. The traditional patient pathway is broken - patients are often in denial about symptoms and avoid physician visits. Our solution bypasses this resistance by empowering concerned spouses to initiate the diagnostic process.

Strategic Insight

Spouses are the "first responders" to OSA symptoms but lack the knowledge and tools to take action. By educating and empowering them, we create a new diagnostic pathway that circumvents patient resistance and drives market growth.

Core Marketing Strategy

The "Silent Alarm" Concept

We reframe OSA symptoms from mere annoyances to critical warning signs of serious health risks - positioning spouse concern as a potentially life-saving "Silent Alarm" that demands attention.

Five Strategic Pillars

Awareness & Education

Transform OSA into a recognizable household term through consistent messaging about symptoms and serious health consequences.

Empathetic Engagement

Address spouses with understanding, validating their concerns and positioning action as an act of love rather than criticism.

Frictionless Pathway

Create a simplified diagnostic journey from awareness to home sleep testing without physician referral barriers.

Precision Targeting

Leverage digital platforms to reach spouses based on demographics, behaviors, and health interests.

Trust Building

Establish credibility through physician partnerships, real patient stories, and transparent information.

Target Audience Persona

Primary: "Concerned Carla"

  • Female, 45-65 years old
  • Married/partnered with children possibly grown
  • Notices partner's snoring, fatigue, and mood changes
  • Researches health information online
  • Values family health and quality of life
  • Seeks solutions but avoids confrontation

Execution Plan

Phased Rollout Strategy

Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)

Website development, content creation, physician partnerships, and initial audience research.

Phase 2: Soft Launch (Month 3)

Limited digital campaign in test markets, A/B testing of messaging, and conversion optimization.

Phase 3: National Launch (Month 4-6)

Full-scale multi-channel campaign rollout with targeted digital, social, and streaming TV ads.

Phase 4: Optimization (Month 7-9)

Performance analysis, channel optimization, and introduction of referral programs.

Phase 5: Expansion (Month 10-12)

Campaign extension to secondary audiences and introduction of advanced educational content.
